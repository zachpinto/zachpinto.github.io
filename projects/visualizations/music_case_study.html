03 — Recommendations
Meridian Records · Annual ReviewRecommendations for Next Quarter03 / 03
Three changes. All data-backed.
Each follows a consistent pattern across 11,247 campaigns — not a single outlier.
REC 01
Restore Spotlight to 63%+ of budget
  • Spotlight budget share fell 63.2% → 55.8% across the year
  • CVR, SPL, and intent rate all declined in exact lockstep
  • Spotlight delivers 2.2× higher CVR and 1.6× higher SPL than Banner at lower cost per listener
  • As Spotlight share fell, Super listener share dropped 42% → 36% — audience quality declined with it
REC 02
Shift budget toward Latin American markets
  • MX delivers 5.8% CVR at €0.086/listener — nearly 2× the CVR of US
  • CL and AR show SPL of 10.4 — highest in the dataset
  • US receives 36% of total budget at €0.271/listener — the worst efficiency of any major market
  • Latin American Spotlight campaigns cost 2.1× less per listener than equivalent US/EU campaigns
REC 03
Prioritize album campaigns over singles
  • 18 of top-20 standout campaigns were albums
  • Spotlight Album: SPL 13.2 vs Spotlight Single: SPL 3.5 — 3.8× deeper engagement
  • Albums cost €0.137/listener vs singles at €0.191/listener
  • Singles received 64% of combined album+single budget this year
Data notes & limitations · No control group — all metrics are absolute, not incremental; causal lift from organic activity cannot be isolated. · No targeting goal column — campaign goals are inferred from dominant audience segment and may not be fully accurate. · Audience segment columns have significant nulls (10–20%); coverage varies by goal type. · Standout campaign threshold of €500 applied to exclude statistically unreliable small campaigns. · Budget figures reflect delivered spend; minor rounding discrepancies exist across data sources.
