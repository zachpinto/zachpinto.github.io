Fall 2017

What is Calculus? Great question. Put simply, it's the "study of change". The reason it's a major 'backbone' of mathematics, and often Calculus is a fundamental mathematical tool that helps us understand change and optimization. Businesses rely on calculus for cost minimization, revenue maximization, and decision-making in constrained environments.

In this post, we'll explore an important technique in **multivariable calculus** called **Lagrange Multipliers**, used for **constrained optimization** problems.

Understanding Optimization and Constraints

Optimization problems often involve finding maximum or minimum values of functions. In a **business setting**, we might want to **minimize production costs** or **maximize profits**. However, businesses also have **constraints**, such as resource availability, budgets, or capacity limitations.

Step 2: Introducing Constraints

Now, consider a business producing two products: **Deluxe Widgets** (\(x\)) and **Eco Widgets** (\(y\)). The company wants to minimize its production cost:

    C(x, y) = 10x² + 4xy + 12y² - 500x - 600y + 400,000

But they must meet a **total production constraint**:

    x + y = 500

Step 3: Solving with Lagrange Multipliers

The method of **Lagrange Multipliers** allows us to solve this efficiently by introducing a new variable \( \lambda \) and solving:

    ∇C = λ∇g

This leads us to the **optimal mix of Deluxe and Eco Widgets** that minimizes cost.

Step 4: Economic Meaning of Lagrange Multipliers

The **Lagrange multiplier** \( \lambda \) represents the "shadow price," or how much the cost would decrease if we could slightly relax the constraint.

This method provides real-world insight into **cost management** and **resource allocation** in business strategy.
